Robotic Process Automation (RPA) vs IT Automation vs Traditional Automation

What is robotic process automation?

RPA is a demand for technology pointed at automating business processes.

In RPA, a firm can configure software or a robot to capture and interpret applications for preparing a transaction, managing data, triggering responses and interacting with other digital services, according to Institute for Robotic Process Automation.

Formation of the term "automation" may make one confuse RPA with ML and AI.

RPA can involve ML or AI, but it is governed by set business logic and structured data, and its rules don't deviate, whereas ML and AI technologies can be trained to make judgments about unstructured inputs.

RPA plot span a broad spectrum varying from something as plain as generating an automatic reply to an email to managing thousands of bots, each programmed to complete a specific task, to automate tasks in an ERP system.

Insurers use RPA to pipe system management data into a claims processing request, rather than having humans type them in from their workstations.

What is IT Automation?

IT automation is the use of guidance to create a repeated process that replaces an IT professional's manual work in data centres and cloud deployments. Software tools, frameworks and tools conduct the tasks with minimum administrator interference. The scope of IT automation varies from individual actions to discrete sequences also, eventually, to an autonomous IT deployment that makes claims based on user behaviour and different event triggers.

Companies today are more turning to IT automation as a way that saves time and improves accuracy, among other benefits. IT automation can apply to a number of different methods, from configuration control to security and alerting. Regardless of what type of technology help you offer—whether it’s managed print services, value-added reselling, internal IT, or managed services—there’s always room for automation within your company.

What is traditional automation

traditional automation is the automation of repeated jobs. It involves application combination at a database level which will require minimal human involvement. IT Automation and Traditional Automation is almost the same concept.

Robotic Process Automation (RPA) vs IT Automation vs Traditional Automation:

Robotic method automation ultimately meant to make jobs more accessible for people, not replace them completely. same as IT Process automation, So, what’s the distinction?

Initially, let’s take a more in-depth approach to what RPA is and how it works. RPA gets its name from the “robot” technology that operates it. This technology can be programmed to work routine, repetitive duties that were once handled by humans. Let’s say an office worker spends 2-3 hours per day doing manual labour, such as data entry or batching. RPA could be programmed to handle these tasks without the requirement for human input. Instead of a robot replacing that human operator, it would be more like a robot assisting them. Those additional hours could then be used on other, more important tasks.

While both RPA and ITPA operate under related concepts, the difference typically lies in how each is used as well as their complexity. Soon, the robotic process industrialisation is primarily being used more broadly at an end-user level. For instance, office workers that may not be well-versed in programming and other complicated IT tasks can leverage RPA for many of their days to day tasks. This can significantly increase their productivity by allowing them to focus on more critical job functions that cannot be automated.

Traditional automation is the computerisation of any type of repetitive task. It is usually found in an outcomes workflow but can be as simple as a process for screen scraping. It entails application combination at a database or infrastructure level and can take months to implement.
