AUTOMATION IN DEFENCE
SIDEWINDER MISSILES
  • Sidewinder missiles is a guided missile.
  • The infrared seeker is sensible to engine heat.
  • A human being is accountable for finding and recognizing the target, properly aiming the missile so that its heat-seeking eye can lock onto the target, and then firing the missile.
COUNTERMEASURES
  • A bundle of little explosives which is at the tail end of aircraft.
  • Used to defeat incoming sidewinder missile fires by enemy planes or anti-aircraft equipment.
UAV DRONES
  • It’s an unmanned air vehicle.
  • It's managed by a computer provided with virtual controls.
  • It is a mobile one and can be launched by using both ships or a ground vehicle
  • The missile launched will be under the control of machine within certain limit i.e it cannot be missed beyond a certain distance
RADAR ANTENNA
  • Recognizes incoming missiles, aircraft, choppers.
  • Gives location of the hostile object.
SCUD MISSILE
  • Its too guided missile.
  • It is programmed to destroy a selected target.
  • It’s equipped with rockets which allow for attacking attacked enemy territory where the manned aircraft are weaker.
AUTOMATION IN MINING
AUTOMATED DRILLING
  • RCS Controlled drilling advances drilling rates and economy and also delivers rapid fault discovery and lower maintenance expenses, higher drill rig availability and utilization
AUTOMATED EXPLORATION
  • RCS Controlled drilling advances drilling rates and economy, and more delivers rapid fault discovery and lower maintenance costs, more powerful drill rig availability and Utilisation.
AUTOMATED TRUCK
  • Independent load haul dump (LHD) and truck haulage operations in open pit and buried mines.
  • These vehicles have onboard ability and perception technologies and GPS which optimize fuel consumption, improve tire life by regular driving models, and minimize collisions.
  • The result is a safer mine and a more efficient fleet
  • Sensor merging: Merging information from different sensors for use on board the vehicle
  • Communications: Handling communication and coordination among multiple agents in the presence of incomplete and imperfect information
  • Motion plan (also called Path planning): Determining an optimal route for the vehicle to go while meeting certain goals and constraints, such as obstacles.
  • Task Allocation and Scheduling: Discovering the optimal distribution of tasks amongst a group of agents, with time and material constraints
AUTOMATION IN AGRICULTURE
  • Agriculture points to the generation of high-quality food and raw materials.
  • Rational use of natural sources and preservation of the environment. In order to complete this modern machinery should be used for difficult agricultural processes and execute difficult works at high efficiencies.
  • For this a large quantity of information has to transmitted by sensors and stored and prepared in the data logging system.
AUTOMATION IN MEDICAL
Swarms of microscopic robots for medical diagnosis
  • Microscopic robots, of sizes similar to bacteria, could move through the smallest blood vessels Thus the robots could pass within a few cell widths of most cells in large organisms via their circulatory systems to perform a variety of biological study and medical tasks. For instance, robots and nanoscale-structured materials inside the body could significantly improve disease examination and treatment
  • Nanorobotics is an emerging area creating machines or robots whose elements are at or close to the scale of a nanometer.Potential applications for nanorobotics in specific include early diagnosis and targeted drug delivery for cancer,[ biomedical instrumentation, medicine, pharmacokinetics monitoring of diabetes, and health care.
Technologies in the Operating Room
  • Robots do not really replace humans, they improve their ability to operate through tiny incisions that are being made AESOP A robotic arms, whose base was rigidly attached to the operating table rail and which was covered with a sterile drape, thereby preserving the incapacity of the operating environment. The arm gave 7 degrees of freedom with four active joints, two passive joints, and one that could be improved statically.
AUTOMATION IN WORKPLACE
Employee analytics
  • Intelligent automation systems excel at wading through deep pools of data to pull out important insights.
  • Software solutions like Gusto, Culture Amp, and Luminoso help HR leaders make sense of vast numbers of employee data to improve employee happiness year-round (instead of waiting for the annual review). Big-name corporations like Johnson & Johnson, Roche, and Hulu are already using these tools to better understand and engage their global teams.
Hiring process
  • Hiring is one of the toughest and most critical responsibilities for any business—but sorting through a sea of applications can keep your HR pros chained to their desks for days or even weeks.
  • This hiring tools can read through applications at marvelous speeds, searching for specific keywords to quickly identify the best applicants from thousands of applications. According to CareerBuilder, 71% of employers said automated applicant tracking software helped enhance the candidate experience. Automated hiring and recruitment tools such as Mya, Smashfly, and BreezyHR use AI to significantly reduce the time to hire and quicken the screening process.
Meetings
  • In offices big and small, meeting connections can be tricky. Scheduling, locating the perfect space, and making sure the right people show up—it’s a juggling act.
  • Tools like Doodle put an end to slow group emails so you can easily connect availability and find a time that works for everyone, even if you have members in different timezones.
