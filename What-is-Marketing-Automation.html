What is Marketing Automation

What is Marketing Automation

Credit: Google

“Marketing automation refers to software platforms and technologies designed for marketing departments and organizations to more effectively market on multiple channels online (such as email, social media, websites, etc.) and automate repetitive tasks”

Marketing automation is described as the process of applying tools and technology to automate repetitive marketing jobs, follow and measure operations performance, increase productivity, and drive performance by minimizing manual actions.

Automating marketing tasks enables the team to spend more of their time on creative and decision-centric tasks such as marketing strategy, workflow planning, customer journey mapping etc.

According to the 2019 House of Marketing report, 48% of marketers are using at least 1 form of marketing automation, and a 2019 Markets and Markets research projects this industry from $3.3 B to $6.4 B in a matter of 5 years between 2019-2024.

Every customer matters. But when you run a business, you might not have time to create a one-off campaign each time you need to talk to your customers. Using marketing automation you can build relationships plus your business

Types Of Marketing Automation Softwares

  1. Customer relationship management (CRM): This kind of automation software stores, manages and helps retrieve customer/ lead data, purchase history and other business interactions between the brand and customers. This data is critical for aiming to plan and orchestrate customer journeys and bring some degree of personalization into their experience.
  2. Email marketing: This is one of the first and most common areas of automation adopted by companies, given the sheer use of this marketing method due to affordability and evergreen impact.
  3. Social Media Posting and Analytics: While Facebook, Twitter and other respective social media platforms provide free analytics for your account, there is an increased business appetite for scheduling multiple posts, deeper insights and connected, omnichannel social media analytics that is user/ customer-centric and not platform-centric only.
  4. Digital Ads and Retargeting: Digital ads are text and multimedia ads on search engines, social media sites and ad networks that are real-time and programmatically managed. Marketing automation now allows you to orchestrate and activate ads across multiple channels and managed using a demand-side platform (DSP).

Pros:

  • Staff satisfaction

    Technology has created marketing and media further interesting. Getting to grips with automation might just keep the soul of the poor email marketing executive who spends his each date setting up email shots.

  • Extra messaging, more extra time, extra channels, more extra sales

    A simple thing really. You can communicate more and in multiple marketing and comms channels, by email, social media, SMS, personalised websites, search engines etc. In theory (once it’s set up) you will save time, at least on the theoretical manual work involved in crafting each word one by one.

  • Testing

    Automation technology is a great platform within which you can test creative in various channels. In leading the same way that AdWords copy is perfected, you can split test efficiently in other channels.

Cons:

  • Extremely enough messaging?

    There’s a risk that automation leads companies to communicate with their customers too often. Companies cannot be connected all of the time, only when a client wants to (whether they know it or not) hear from them.

  • It’s complex

    By the time, a lot of marketing automation consists of pathways of triggered emails, normally triggered by browsing and purchase habits on a website or communication with using email.
    These technics can be long-winded while setting up, though email marketing service providers usually practice in this and to some degree can ‘copy’ standard pathways (e.g. welcome campaign) between clients.
