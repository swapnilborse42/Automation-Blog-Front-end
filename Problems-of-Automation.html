Problems of Automation

Problems of Automation

“Bill Gates has quoted, “Automation applied to an inefficient operation will magnify the inefficiency”, and this happens to be the biggest disadvantage of automation. A slight mistake while scripting might result in the mistake getting endlessly repeated, until its detection and rectification.”

Credit: pixabay

Not everything can be automated

There are certain restrictions on process automation, especially when it comes to businesses. Take for example client communications. While the initial contact can be put on autopilot to a certain amount, either using email automation or Chabot’s, further communication is extremely tough to automate.

Plus, only a human can experience another human’s doubts and worries and, as a result, find the proper strategy to address them. No AI agent is capable of understanding social psychology and react to the subtle changes in their behaviour in a proper way.

People still prefer human touch over the insensitive robots, when it comes to customer support and sales.

Capital expenditure

While automation can be highly efficient and offer a positive ROI, it may also require a very high cost of money. That is why, before making a decision, When calculating ROI, it is important to include an increase in performance, reduction of labour costs and reduction of defects/withdrawals, together with capital expenditures before deciding whether or not an argument exists. Gets rid of jobs

It is true that with the initiation of automation there are some jobs that can become redundant, but this does not necessarily have to be a negative relationship of automation. Instead of the workers performing numbing, monotonous or unpleasant tasks, you can receive instruction to transfer to work in other areas of your company. Many companies have discovered that after automation was installed, they saw an improvement in sales, creating more jobs in different parts of their profession.

Bespoke automation becomes redundant when production processes change

As with any type of machinery tools, if you change your production process or product that you are manufacturing so that a particular machine is no large part of the process, the machine will become repetitive. Therefore, it is important for the coming test of any automation installed in your production method. A qualified automation business will design its automation system to enable it to easily adapt to changes in product configuration or production process. For example; Using standard flexible automation, such as robots, can easily be used anywhere else in the production process, even if the existing process becomes redundant.

skilled maintenance department

A experienced resources department is often required to service and maintain the automation method in proper working form. Failure to control the automation system will eventually result in lost production and/or bad parts being built.

Lengthy onboarding

While they’re automated, performing storage and retrieval tasks with minimal human intervention, AS/RS solutions must be programmed to follow the necessary routes and retrieve and store the correct items in the right locations. Operators require training to operate the system effectively

Automated systems need a high level of testing

Another concern is that automated systems need a high level of testing and quality control to ensure that the automated process is completely free of errors. This requires to be done because of automation any slight error has a chance of increased magnification.

Incompatible with Customization

With automation grows a loss of versatility or flexibility. A machine can only do a restricted scope of tasks, only performing what it is programmed to do, unlike people, who can do a variety of jobs. Automation entails repeating the same method over and over again, often requiring uniformity. One-off customization for selective customers is incompatible with some automated processes.
