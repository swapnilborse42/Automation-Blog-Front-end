Education and training for Automation technician

Education and training for Automation technician

Automation technicians require a high school diploma or GED, though many employers prefer an associate's degree in a related field. Certification is possible for a specific job as well as for pieces of material from specific manufacturers.

Essential Information

Automation technicians service and maintain these computer-controlled operations and robotic machines used within industrial and industrial facilities to reduce human interference and maximize efficiency. Their jobs need an understanding of electronics, mechanics and computers. Employers may prefer candidates that have obtained an associate degree; optional industry certifications are also available.

Required Education- Associate degree may be preferred

Other Requirements- Optional certifications available from the Electronics Technicians Association and Manufacturing Skill Standards Council

Preferred Skills- Strong math, computer and electronics skills; ability to work with hand tools and various instruments

Projected Job Growth (2018-2028)- 1% for all electro-mechanical technicians

Median Salary (2018)- $57,790 for all electro-mechanical technicians

Requirements to Become an Automation Technician

Employers may prefer applicants who have completed an automation technology or mechatronics associate degree program. Those degree specifications integrate computer science among mechanics, electronics and method controls. They more usually involve coursework in hydraulics, pneumatics, programmable logic controllers, electrical circuits, electrical machinery and human-machine interfaces. Stable math and analytic abilities are necessary to understand automated systems and isolate problems. Operators are expected to become proficient with various instruments and hand tools, such as electrical multimeters.

Certifications can enhance employment opportunities and keep professionals current with the latest technological developments. Credential programs are offered by several equipment manufacturers and professional associations, such as the Electronics Technicians Association and the Manufacturing Skill Standards Council.

Our best bet is to study a course which includes a lot of in-depth control systems, electrical machines like motors and generators and some embedded systems. Understanding in certain domains of mechanical engineering like inertia, dynamics, forward and inverse kinematics, plus vibration analysis is recommended. A fundamental understanding of robotics course is highly recommended, though this is possible to get knowledge on this from online material.

This apart, hands-on experience with SCADA, PLC, software like LabView and industrial software like Siemens TIA portal, ABB, GE, Rockwell, Parker Hannifin, Kuka, Delta etc. will help you get a head start.

Should some knowledge about G-code and M-code used in CNC controllers. Basic C programming will benefit in developing software for programmable automation controllers applying the widely used industrial programming languages like IEC-61131.

3D design understanding using AutoCAD or any other software is good.

Definitely, a part of patience and adaptability is needed as each project involves a lot of different engineers, technicians and specialists, and usually goes very gently. Speak to the Shop floor operators to understand what the real problem is the managers mostly will not know the technical difficulties. If thou love to travel, people you are going to love your work.
