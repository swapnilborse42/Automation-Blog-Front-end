Legal issues in Automation

Whether using RPA as part of a managed service or by an in-house option due attention will be necessary. Remarkable issues to be considered:

  • What information will the robots have access to and has data security/data safety been examined
  • Duty, Accountability and Liability Issues: While machines are taking choices and a meaningful autonomy and knowledge are included in the device, the questions are being asked about who will be accountable, accountable and liable for the actions taken at the fly. A simple case of this is the automated car. Among all the safety measures in position, there is yet a remote possibility of an autonomous vehicle involved into an accident. Now if that occurs - because as per law which should be accountable for this happening?
  • During important processes, does there business continuity in place if the digital workforce is not ready to operate ?
  • What is the worst-case scenario and what mitigants are in place to avoid these
  • Secrecy and Safety: Anything about the privacy and security of the data underuse? Whatever are the compliance standards of the proposed pilot and full-fledged implementation? Is the end-user about whom the information is being collected, also aware of the condition plus possible implications of this data collection? Does he/she comfortable signing for privacy standards? Has the design taken into evidence of issues like the right to deletion, the power to be neglected, data portability etc.?
  • What commercial interests will RPA make and are they/can they be secured in contractually.
  • Do the tasks to be performed by the “robots” repeatable and can they be mapped.
  • Whatever is the people impact of the use of RPA technology: implies there a risk of redundancy for those currently arranging the work, do parts need to be re-profiled.
  • Does the RPA use in a controlled sector, e.g business services; if then, are the regulatory necessities applicable to that sector being complied with (e.g. SYSC 8 in the financial services sector)
  • Automated contracts: Now, the contracts are administered human to human. As section of a human to human contact, the completed and signed between humans. While everything moves to a machine to machine, what issues if machines start executing a deal between them? Would robots have the authority to execute such agreements? Can all be a challenge in the court of law if there is a dispute
  • Will the ‘adopter’ be capable to exit the RPA technology, without disturbing ongoing processes
