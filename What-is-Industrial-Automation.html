What is Industrial Automation?

What is Industrial Automation?

Industrial Automation is the method of control systems, such as computers or robots, and learning technologies for handling different processes and machinery in an industry to replace an individual.

Industrial Automation helps to increase the product quality, reliability and production scale while reducing generation and design cost by adopting new, innovative and integrated technologies and services.

In an abstract, industrial automation can be defined as the use of set technologies and automatic control devices that results in the automatic operation and control of industrial methods without significant human interference and achieving better performance than manual administration. These automation devices consist of PLCs, PCs, PACs, etc. and technologies include various industrial communication systems.

Industrial automation is the second level beyond mechanization in the scope of industrialization.

Earlier the idea of automation was to enhance productivity even automated systems can work 24 hours a day and reduce the cost associated with human operators like wages. However, today, the focus of automation has changed to increasing quality and flexibility in the manufacturing method.

Hierarchy of an Industrial Automation System:

Industrial automation systems can be complicated in nature, having a large number of devices working in synchronization with automation technologies.

Control Level:

This level consists of various industrialisation devices like CNC machines, PLCs, etc., which takes the process parameters from various sensors.

Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs): PLCs are the main heart of the automation system. PLC is the controller used to control the plant or process or system or machine.

Supervising and Production Control Level:

In this level, automatic machines and monitoring system facilitates the controlling and intervening functions like Human Machine Interface (HMI), SCADA etc.

SCADA extensively used in the Process industry for remote monitoring and control of the plant/ system which is geographically wide spread.

Advantages of Industrial Automation

Industrial automation reduces the operational cost by eliminating healthcare costs and paid leave and holidays associated with a human operator.

High productivity

Even many companies hire hundreds of production workers and works for the maximum number of hours, the plant still needs to be closed for maintenance and holidays. Industrial automation helps to achieve the aim of the company by allowing the company to run a manufacturing plant for 24 hours in a day, 7 days in a week and 365 days a year. This begins with a significant improvement in the productivity of the company.

High Quality

The error associated with a human being avoided with the replacing machines. Further, unlike human beings, robots do not involve any error, which results in products with uniform quality manufactured.

High flexibility

Appending a new job in the assembly line needs training with a human operator, however, robots can be programmed to do any task. This makes the manufacturing process more flexible.

High Information Accuracy

Adding automated data collection can allow you to collect key production information, improve data accuracy, and reduce data collection costs. This gives you with the facts to make the right resolutions when it comes to reducing waste and improving your processes.

High safety

Robots handle hazardous condition so industrial automation can make the production line safe for the employees.Because of these benefits automation has recently found more acceptance from many automation companies

Disadvantages of Industrial Automation

  • Many workers lost their jobs
  • Initial machinery cost is high
  • Time to Time Maintenance check is essential
  • Need to teach the workers how the machinery works (some machineries are complex like CNC’s )

Examples of Industrial Automation

  • CNC machining
  • Pick and place robots
  • Pump stations
  • Vision systems for parts inspection
